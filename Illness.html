ILLNESSES THAT CAN AFFECT BY THIS SOCIAL ISSUE

GASTROINTESTINAL INFECTIONS

Gastrointestinal infections are a common global health problem, often affecting the stomach or intestines and typically resulting in diarrhea. Most cases resolve without treatment after a few days. However, in select populations, diarrheal diseases can carry a high degree of morbidity and mortality.

ASTHMA

Asthma is a long-term inflammatory disease of the airways of the lungs. It is characterized by variable and recurring symptoms, reversible airflow obstruction, and easily triggered bronchospasms. Symptoms include episodes of wheezing, coughing, chest tightness, and shortness of breath.

FOOD POISONING

SKIN DISEASES

Skin diseases are conditions that affect the skin, causing various symptoms such as rashes, inflammation, itchiness, or other skin changes. These diseases can be caused by a combination of genetic, lifestyle, and environmental factors.

HEPATITIS

Hepatitis is inflammation of the liver tissue. Some people or animals with hepatitis have no symptoms, whereas others develop yellow discoloration of the skin and whites of the eyes (jaundice), poor appetite, vomiting, tiredness, abdominal pain, and diarrhea.

TOXIC EXPOSURE

Vector-borne diseases are human illnesses caused by parasites, viruses, and bacteria that are transmitted by vectors. These vectors can be mosquitoes, ticks, fleas, or other insects that feed on human blood or other substances. Vector-borne diseases are a significant public health concern, as they can cause serious illness, disability, and even death.
