IMPROPER WASTE DISPOSAL

Through education and advocacy, we can empower people to adopt responsible waste management practices, such as reducing, reusing, and recycling waste materials. By taking small yet meaningful actions in our daily lives, such as properly disposing of trash, reducing single-use plastics, and participating in clean-up efforts, we can collectively make a significant difference in preserving our environment for future generations.Together, let's raise awareness and take action to address improper waste disposal and build a cleaner, healthier, and more sustainable world for all.

REUSE

Reusing involves the repeated use of items or parts of items which still have usable aspects. To find new ways to use things that otherwise would have been thrown out.

REDUCE

Reduce means to cut back on the amount of trash we generate. Eitherway on choosing how to use things with care to reduce the amount of waste generated.

RECYCLE

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. This concept often includes the recovery of energy from waste materials.

FOR A HAPPIER EARTH

By reducing, reusing, and recycling, we decrease air and water pollution from waste disposal, conserve materials for continuous reuse in making new products, reduce demand for mining and extraction of virgin materials. By doing this will benefit us from having a clean environment that will be good for our health.
