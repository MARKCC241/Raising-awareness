EFFECTS OF IMPROPER WASTE DISPOSAL

WATER CONTAMINATE

Water contamination refers to the presence of harmful substances or pollutants in water that can negatively impact human health, wildlife, and the environment. These contaminants can come from various sources, including industrial waste, agricultural runoff, improper waste disposal, and natural phenomena such as volcanic eruptions or earthquakes.

AIR POLUTION

Air pollution is contamination of the indoor or outdoor environment by any chemical, physical or biological agent that modifies the natural characteristics of the atmosphere. Household combustion devices, motor vehicles, industrial facilities and forest fires are common sources of air pollution.

ANIMAL IMPACT

Improper disposal of animal waste can lead to the spread of diseases and contamination of soil and water sources, which can have adverse effects on the health of animals and the environment. Animals can also contract diseases and illness from improper waste disposal, threatening their health and well-being.

FLOODING

Improper waste disposal, particularly of garbage and plastics, poses a significant threat to drainage systems, often leading to blockages that can result in flooding. In areas like Malabon, this issue is particularly pronounced, as blocked drainage canals exacerbate the risk of flooding.

HEALTH RISK

Improper waste disposal can have serious health impacts for several reasons. When waste is not disposed properly, it can lead to the contamination of air, soil, and water. This contamination can result in various health problems for humans, including respiratory issues, infections, and skin diseases.

ENVIRONMENT

The improper disposal of waste can lead to a harmful effects including water contamination, air pollution, negative impacts on wildlife, increased flooding, and heightened health risks, The issues not only harm our ecosystem and biodiversity but also contribute to the exacerbation of climate change.
